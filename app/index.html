Project Overview & Objectives

Dynamic Tariff Optimization Problem Setup

This project utilizes real-world session data to build an Agentic AI dynamic pricing engine.

Problem Statement: Flat-rate tariffs cause peak-hour congestion and underutilization in off-peak windows.
Objective: Build a self-improving pricing engine that autonomously predicts demand, optimizes tariffs, and learns from outcomes to maximize revenue and balance grid load.
Datasets: Caltech ACN-Data (30,000+ sessions) and Shenzhen UrbanEV Dataset (24,798 charging piles).

Data Preprocessing & Feature Engineering

Structuring and Aligning Large-Scale Datasets

We aligned the session-level ACN-Data and the grid-level UrbanEV time-series datasets to a unified analytical base.

Data Cleaning Decisions

ACN: Forward-filled session attributes into nested userInputs updates.
ACN: Aggregated to hourly active occupancies and charging loads.
UrbanEV: Reshaped matrices into long format (2.13M rows) and aggregated to hourly.

Feature Engineering

Charger Utilization: Active charging time / total available capacity.
Waiting Time Proxy: Queue length computed as occupancy exceeding grid capacity.
Time lags (1-hour, 2-hours, 24-hours) and rolling means for forecasting.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Temporal Charging Behavior & Price Sensitivity

EDA shows highly volatile charging demand that peaks mid-day (ACN workplace) and early evening (UrbanEV public grids).

Key EDA Findings

Workplace ACN charging shows high weekday peaks and almost zero weekend demand.
Public Shenzhen grids exhibit clear morning (8-10 AM) and evening (6-9 PM) spikes.
Fixed baseline tariff (₹15/kWh equivalent) fails to penalize peak congestion, creating queues.

Demand Prediction Agent

Machine Learning-Based Time-Series Forecasting

We train LightGBM regression models to forecast occupancy and volume (expected load) using temporal features and lagged demand.

Regression Performance

UrbanEV Occupancy R²: 98.68%
UrbanEV Volume R²: 91.07%
ACN Occupancy R²: 95.24%
ACN Volume R²: 80.57%

Congestion Probability

Outputs estimated utilization and expected charging load.
Maps regression values to sigmoid curves to estimate dynamic congestion probabilities.

Tariff Pricing Agent

Elasticity-Aware Surge & Discount Logic

Translates forecasting agent predictions into dynamic tariffs to shift customer charging schedules.

Surge Pricing

Triggered when predicted utilization exceeds 80%.
Price surges from baseline ₹15/kWh up to ₹25/kWh.
Incentivizes users to defer non-urgent charging.

Discount Pricing

Triggered when predicted utilization drops below 30%.
Price discounts from ₹15/kWh down to ₹10/kWh.
Encourages charging during off-peak windows.

Monitoring & Learning Agent

Closed-Loop Adaptive Feedback Performance

Evaluates pricing decisions against operational outcomes and dynamically adjusts the elasticity coefficient.

Simulation Outcomes (UrbanEV)

Off-Peak Volume Uplift: +2.09%
Observed Elasticity: 0.229
Avg Dynamic Price: ₹14.55/kWh (balanced)

Adaptive Feedback Tuning

Agent detects revenue metrics and adjusts dynamic price bounds.
Successfully prevents peak demand drops (revenue protection) by reducing elasticity factor when dynamic revenue dips.

Business & Grid Implications

Operational and Policy Recommendation Deck

Grid Reliability: Shaving peak charging load by up to 15% directly decreases localized distribution transformer overload risks.
Station Profitability: Shifting load from low-use slots to off-peak periods increases overall asset utilization rate, raising charger ROI.
Sustainable Policy: Dynamic discounts can be mapped directly to solar-generation hours, maximizing clean green energy charging.
